### Phase: 1. Unpacking the Initial Pull

### February 23, 2026

#### Voice Note Reflection: The One-On-One Core
**Theme:** [1:1 discussions, product-hiring, deep-context]

**Source:** Voice notes/transcripts, late night exploration with Claude.

*"I think I really like to work one-on-one with people so I could really help in the hiring challenges."*

**The Initial Insight:**
Hiring is treated as a transactional, scale problem by the market, but the actual problem isn't size – it's depth.
- **The JD Illusion:** What companies *write* down as their requirement vs what they *actually* need to solve right now.
- **The Urgency Illusion:** Is the job post actually urgent, or is it just sitting there?
- **The Candidate Alignment:** Many candidates, but what do they truly want? Are they truly an A-player for *this exact moment* in the company's lifecycle?

**Observation:**
The traditional system expects static documents (JDs, Resumes) to match a dynamic reality. Companies change. Candidates change. This is the root problem. 

**What resonates internally:**
A completely independent, solo-led model ("answerable to no one"). This aligns intensely with my need to:
1. Not play standard games (not building an "agency" or managing recruiters).
2. Follow my deepest flow state (1:1 deep work with people on top of their game).
3. Do grounded action (as per the Nov 19 2025 revelation). 

Starting domains: Product Manager, Product Designer, Product Data. This creates a clean boundary for the exploration.

### April 27, 2026

#### The LinkedIn Feed as the Real Signal Source
**Theme:** [signal-sourcing, network-anchor, warm-outreach, tool-design]

**Source:** Conversation while thinking through AI automation for this project.

**The Insight:**
The right signal isn't job boards — it's the **LinkedIn feed**. Because people post hiring needs there, and the feed is personalized to your network. This means:
- The person who posted the job is visible and traceable
- You may already know them, or know someone who does
- That makes outreach **warm by default**, not cold

This is the core differentiator from any generic recruiting workflow. The anchor is: *who posted this job?* — not just what the job says.

**The Information Asymmetry this solves:**
Standard job boards strip out the human context. The feed preserves it. A post like "we're finally hiring a PM, been looking for 6 months" tells you urgency, frustration, and relationship entry point — none of which appears on a JD.

**What the agent should actually do:**
1. Monitor LinkedIn feed (personalized to Pranav's account — requires authenticated access)
2. Flag job-related posts from people in the network
3. Cross-reference: does Pranav know this person? Do they have a mutual connection?
4. Deliver a briefing via Telegram — Pranav acts on LinkedIn himself
5. Agent drafts the outreach/context; human sends it

**Why Telegram as the delivery layer:**
Two-way channel. Agent sends briefings while Pranav sleeps. Pranav can reply with commands ("research this company", "draft a note to this founder"). Keeps LinkedIn actions human, which protects the account.

**Open questions:**
- How to authenticate LinkedIn feed access without account ban risk (read-only browser automation vs. email digest parsing)
- How to build the "do I know this person?" cross-reference (connections list export vs. manual recognition)
- Is the primary job finding roles for candidates, or finding candidates for roles — or both?

⭐ **Breakthrough:** The product isn't "a hiring service." It's a **network intelligence layer** — surfacing warm, high-signal hiring moments from your existing relationships before anyone else sees them.
